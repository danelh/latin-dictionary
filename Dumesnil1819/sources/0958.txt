+Ephebus+, (_from_  ἐπὶ _and_ ἥβη, _youth_) %a young man%, %a youth%. According to Censorinus, young men were placed amongst the _ephebi_ from the age of fifteen to seventeen.
* Nam is postquam excessit ex ephebis. #Ter.#
* Greges epheborum. #Cic.#
+Pubes+ or +Puber+, %one of the age of puberty%, which was fourteen for boys, and twelve for girls.
* Puber setas. #Liv.#
* Pater filium puberem habens. #Cic.#
_Pubes_ properly signifies downy, covered with hair.
