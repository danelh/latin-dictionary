+Circa+ and +Circum+ seem to be the same. It is however necessary to observe that _circa_ better denotes, round about, at some distance, and _circum_, near about.
* Cum Rullus Capuam et urbes circa Capuam occuparit. #Cic.#
_Circum_ might not be so accurate. In the same maner
* Templa quæ circum forum sunt. #Id.#
_Circa_ might imply a greater distance.
+Circumcirca+, round about, on every side.
* Cum ab Ægina Megarum versus navigarem, cœpi regiones circumcirca prospicere. #Cic.#
+Circiter+, about, nigh unto.
* Circiter meridiem. #Cæs.# (ad understood).
* Loca hæc circiter excidit mihi. #Plaut.# (per understood) I lost it thereabout.
* In media circiter urbe. #Cæs.#
