+Cædere+, %to beat, to cut, to tear to pieces%.
* Virgis ad necem cædi. #Cic.#
* Januam cædere saxis. #Id.#
_Cædere_, said of stones, signifies %to cut them off% from a quarry; whereas _secare_ is %to saw% them previous to the laying them out to use.
* Marmora secanda locas. #Hor.#
+Secare+, %to cut%.
* Secare varices alicui. #Cic.#
Figuratively:
* Quo multæ magnæque secantur judice lites. #Hor.#
+Resecare+, %to pare%, %to clip%, %to cut or saw off%.
* Resecare linguam, palpebras. #Cic.#
Figuratively:
* Resecare audacias et libidines. #Cic.#
